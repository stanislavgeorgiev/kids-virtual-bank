## Weekly Discussion Topics

### Week 1: Understanding Income
**Topic**: Where does money come from?

**Discussion Points:**
- Work = money (chores = allowance)
- Different jobs pay different amounts
- Skills and effort affect income

**Activity:**
- Review this week's chores
- Calculate total earnings
- Discuss which chores were worth the effort

**Question for Kids:**
*"Which chore gave you the best 'pay per minute'? Would you do it again?"*

---

### Week 2: Tracking Spending
**Topic**: Where does your money go?

**Discussion Points:**
- Every purchase is a choice
- Small purchases add up
- Needs vs wants

**Activity:**
- Review withdrawal history
- Categorize spending (treats, toys, gifts, etc.)
- Calculate totals by category

**Question for Kids:**
*"If you could get that money back, what would you spend it on instead?"*

---

### Week 3: Saving for Goals
**Topic**: Planning for bigger purchases

**Discussion Points:**
- Delayed gratification
- Breaking big goals into small steps
- Staying motivated

**Activity:**
- Set a savings goal (something they want)
- Calculate how many weeks to save
- Create visual tracker

**Question for Kids:**
*"What's something you really want that costs more than you have right now?"*

---

### Week 4: Making Choices
**Topic**: Opportunity cost

**Discussion Points:**
- Every purchase means not buying something else
- Comparing options
- Is it worth it?

**Activity:**
- Present two options (e.g., $10 toy vs saving for $30 game)
- Discuss trade-offs
- Kid makes choice and explains reasoning

**Question for Kids:**
*"If you buy this, what will you NOT be able to buy?"*

## Age-Appropriate Concepts

### Ages 5-7
**Focus**: Basic concepts
- Counting money
- More vs less
- Saving vs spending
- Simple choices

**Activities:**
- Count physical coins/bills
- Match toy prices to savings
- Picture charts for goals
- Sticker rewards for saving

### Ages 8-10
**Focus**: Planning and tracking
- Weekly budgets
- Category tracking
- Goal setting
- Basic percentages

**Activities:**
- Create spending plan
- Track daily spending
- Calculate savings rate
- Compare prices online

### Ages 11-13
**Focus**: Advanced concepts
- Interest (if you add it)
- Opportunity cost
- Long-term planning
- Value of money

**Activities:**
- Research prices
- Compare options
- Plan for large purchase

**Focus**: Real-world preparation
- Real bank account setup
- Credit vs debit concepts

**Activities:**
- Budget for real expenses
- Research bank accounts
- Plan for actual goals
- Transition to real banking

## Behavioral Economics Concepts

### Present Bias
**Concept**: Wanting things now vs later

**Teaching moment**: 
Offer choice: $5 today or $7 next week
Discuss which is "better" financially

### Mental Accounting
**Concept**: Different "buckets" of money

**Activity**:
Create categories: Saving, Spending, Giving
Kid allocates allowance between them

### Sunk Cost Fallacy
**Concept**: Past spending shouldn't affect future choices

**Discussion**:
"I already spent $20 on this game, should I spend $10 more?"
Teach: Only consider future value, not past spending

### Anchoring
**Concept**: First price we see affects judgment

**Activity**:
Show expensive item first, then less expensive one
Discuss how first price made second seem cheap

## Discussion Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The Impulse Buy
**Setup**: Kid wants to spend all money on candy

**Discussion**:
- Will you regret this later?
- What else could you buy?
- How long to earn it back?

### Scenario 2: The Big Goal
**Setup**: Kid wants $200 gaming console

**Discussion**:
- How long to save?
- Can you wait that long?
- What smaller goals first?

### Scenario 3: The Peer Pressure
**Setup**: Friend has new expensive toy

**Discussion**:
- Do you really want it or just because friend has it?
- What makes you happy?
- Different families, different priorities

### Scenario 4: The Mistake
**Setup**: Kid bought something and immediately regretted it

**Discussion**:
- What did you learn?
- How to avoid next time?
- Can you return it?

## Values Alignment

### Giving
**Concept**: Sharing wealth with others

**Activities**:
- Donate portion to charity
- Buy gift for someone in need
- Support cause they care about

**Discussion**:
"How does it feel to help others with your money?"

### Sustainability
**Concept**: Money choices affect the world

**Activities**:
- Compare disposable vs reusable
- Research ethical brands
- Calculate environmental cost

**Discussion**:
"Is the cheapest option always the best?"

### Quality vs Quantity
**Concept**: Sometimes worth paying more

**Activities**:
- Buy quality item that lasts
- Compare cheap vs durable
- Calculate cost per use

**Discussion**:
"Would you rather have 5 cheap toys or 1 good one?"
